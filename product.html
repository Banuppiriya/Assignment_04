Donuts are a sweet, fried pastry that are often ring-shaped and made from flour, sugar, eggs, and milk.
They can be topped with powdered sugar, chocolate, or fruit.


History

The Dutch immigrants of New York in the 17th and 18th centuries made fried dough balls called olie koeken,
which means "oil cakes." Captain Hanson Gregory, a Dutch sailor, is said to have invented the hole in the doughnut.


Types

The most common types of donuts are ring donuts and filled donuts.
Filled donuts can be filled with jelly, cream, custard, or other sweet fillings.
Other shapes of donuts include balls, flattened spheres, and twists.

Nutrition

Donuts are high in fat and calories.
A glazed old-fashioned donut contains about 420 calories, 21 grams of fat, and 57 grams of carbohydrates.

Enjoyment

Donuts are often eaten with coffee or milk.
The sweetness of donuts balances the bitterness of coffee.
